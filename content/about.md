# About Ryan Cole

I've shipped games. Not as a corporate producer with a team of 200,  as an indie developer who wore every hat from game designer to QA lead to the person frantically fixing build pipelines the night before a deadline. Three titles across mobile and PC platforms, each one teaching me something the previous one didn't.

That experience is the foundation of everything on this site. I started GameDevProducer.com because I kept seeing the same gaps in the production content available online: it was either too high-level to be useful or written by people who had never actually had to cut a feature two weeks before ship. Developers and producers at small and mid-size studios need practical frameworks,  not theoretical best practices written for AAA studios with dedicated producers on every discipline.

My goal here is to document what actually works when you're trying to ship a real game with real constraints.

## Why I Started GameDevProducer.com

When I was producing my second game, I spent hours searching for good information on things like how to structure a milestone system that works for a team of five, how to run a production retrospective that actually changes behavior, or how to scope a feature list honestly without demoralizing your team. I found plenty of academic content and GDC talks from big studios,  almost nothing from producers who had done it small.

GameDevProducer.com is the resource I needed back then. Every article is grounded in the reality of independent development, though the frameworks apply broadly across team sizes.

## What You'll Find Here

- **Pre-production:** Scoping, milestone planning, green-light criteria, and risk identification
- **Production management:** Sprint structures, daily standups, bug triage, and team communication
- **Shipping:** Certification, submission, build pipelines, and launch coordination
- **Post-launch:** Live ops basics, patch planning, player feedback loops
- **Industry context:** Market trends, platform dynamics, and publisher relationships
- **Tools and workflows:** Project management software, documentation, and async communication

## A Note on Our Content

Everything on this site reflects my personal experience and research,  it is not a substitute for professional legal, financial, or business advice. Game development outcomes vary enormously based on team, technology, market timing, and dozens of other factors. I link to authoritative sources and first-person accounts wherever possible, and I update articles when the industry shifts.
